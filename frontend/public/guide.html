What is Vestra?

Vestra is a tool for automated token distribution on the Solana blockchain. Think of it like setting up a standing bank transfer — you define who gets paid, how many tokens, and on what schedule, and Vestra handles the rest automatically.

Common use cases:

  • Team vesting — release tokens to co-founders or employees over 4 years
  • Investor payouts — distribute tokens after a cliff period
  • Milestone-based grants — unlock tokens only when a goal is verified
  • Recurring payments — pay contributors weekly or monthly
ℹ️
Vestra runs on Solana Devnet — a test network where tokens have no real value. It's safe to experiment freely.

Step 1 — Connect a Wallet

A wallet is your identity on the blockchain — it's like a bank account address that only you control. You need one to use Vestra.

⚠️
Look for the Connect Wallet button in the top-right corner. It pulses with an orange dot to make it easy to find.

Click Connect Wallet. A small menu appears with two options:

A

Continue with Email

The easiest option — no extra software needed. Enter your email address, receive a one-time code, and Vestra automatically creates a Solana wallet for you behind the scenes. You own it completely.

B

Phantom / Solflare

For users who already have a Solana browser-extension wallet installed. Click this to open the wallet's own connection dialog. If you don't know what this is, use Email instead.

Email login walkthrough

  1. Click Connect Wallet → Continue with Email.
  2. A Privy dialog appears. Type your email and press Continue.
  3. Check your inbox for a 6-digit code and paste it into the dialog.
  4. The dialog closes and the button now shows your email address with a green dot — you are connected.
✅
Once connected, the button shows your email (or a shortened wallet address) and a green dot. You're ready to use Vestra.

Step 3 — Open the Create Form

Click New agreement from the Agreements page, or go directly to /streams/create.

1

Connect your wallet first

If you are not yet connected, the Connect Wallet button is in the top-right of the form page. The Review agreement button at the bottom stays greyed out until you connect.

2

Take the guided tour (optional but recommended)

Click How to use this form? (the blue button below the page title). A step-by-step overlay highlights each field and explains what to type. You can go Next, Back, or Skip at any time.

3

Fill in the form fields

See the Form Fields section below for a detailed explanation of each input.

4

Click Review agreement

A summary card appears showing everything you entered. Read it carefully before confirming.

5

Confirm and send

Click Confirm and send. Your wallet will ask you to approve the transaction. The tokens are locked into the agreement immediately.

Form Field Reference

Every field on the Create Agreement form is explained here.

Recipient wallet required

The Solana wallet address of the person or account that will receive the tokens. It looks like a random string of letters and numbers, e.g. 7xKX...gT3u. Ask the recipient for their public wallet address — it is safe to share. Never paste a private key here.

Token required

The token you want to distribute. Type a token name (e.g. USDC) or ticker in the search box and select it from the dropdown. You can also paste a full token contract address directly. Only tokens you actually hold in your wallet can be streamed.

Amount required

The total number of tokens to lock into this agreement. This full amount is moved from your wallet into escrow immediately when you confirm. It is then released to the recipient according to the payout schedule you choose.

Agreement ID required

A unique number to identify this agreement. It is auto-generated for you — you can leave it as-is. Only change it if you need to link to a specific ID in your own records. Each agreement ID must be unique per wallet.

Start date required

When the payout schedule begins. Tokens start accumulating for the recipient from this moment. The recipient cannot claim anything before this date. Defaults to right now.

Lock until optional

Also called a cliff. Tokens accrue from the Start date but the recipient cannot withdraw anything until this date. Leave blank if you want the recipient to be able to withdraw at any time. Common in vesting: a 1-year cliff means no tokens can be claimed for the first year even though time is counting.

End date required

When the final payout occurs. The payout schedule runs from Start date to End date. All tokens will have been released by this point.

Payout schedule required

How tokens are released over time. Three options:

Option How it works Best for
Even payouts over time Tokens drip continuously and evenly from Start to End. The recipient can withdraw accumlated tokens at any time. Salaries, subscriptions, contributor payments.
Locked period, then even payouts Nothing can be claimed until the Lock until date. After that, tokens release evenly until End date. Investor / team vesting with a cliff.
Release when goals are completed Tokens are divided into milestones. Each milestone unlocks only when a designated verifier approves it. Grants, bounties, project-based contracts.
Allow cancellation of unreleased tokens optional

A checkbox. When ticked, you (the sender) can cancel the agreement at any time and get back the tokens that have not yet been claimed by the recipient. When un-ticked, the agreement is permanent — even you cannot stop it. Tokens already claimable by the recipient are always theirs regardless.

Review & Submit

Once you fill all required fields and click Review agreement, a summary screen replaces the form.

What to check in the summary

  • Recipient — confirm the address is correct. Sending to the wrong address cannot be reversed.
  • Token and amount — make sure these match what you intended.
  • Dates — double-check Start, Lock until, and End dates.
  • Payout schedule — correct type selected.

Buttons on the summary screen

Button What it does
Back to edit Returns you to the form so you can change anything. Nothing is sent yet.
Confirm and send Signs and broadcasts the transaction. Your wallet will show an approval prompt — click Approve/Confirm in your wallet to proceed.
⚠️
Once confirmed, the tokens are locked. The agreement is on-chain and cannot be changed. If you enabled cancellation, you can cancel it later — but you cannot edit dates or amounts.

After submission

A success message appears with a transaction signature (a long hash). You can click it to view the transaction on Solana Explorer. The new agreement will appear on the Agreements page.

Managing Existing Agreements

Go to Agreements to see all agreements where your wallet is the creator or the recipient.

Withdraw (recipient only)

If you are the recipient and tokens have accumulated since the last claim, a Withdraw button appears. Click it to transfer all currently claimable tokens to your wallet. You can withdraw as many times as you like — there is no penalty for claiming early or late.

Verify milestone (milestone agreements only)

If the agreement uses Release when goals are completed, a verifier must approve each milestone before tokens unlock. Click Verify milestone and approve in your wallet. The recipient can then withdraw that portion.

Cancel (creator only, if cancellation was enabled)

Click Cancel next to an agreement. Tokens that the recipient has already earned up to this moment will still be available for them to withdraw. Tokens not yet earned are returned to your wallet immediately.

ℹ️
If you did not enable cancellation when creating the agreement, the Cancel button will not appear. The agreement will run to completion regardless.

Disconnect

Click the button in the top-right that shows your email or wallet address, then click Disconnect. Your agreements are not deleted — they are on-chain permanently. You can reconnect any time to access them again.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need cryptocurrency to use Vestra?

On devnet (the test environment), no. Click Mint mock tokens to get free test tokens. In a production deployment on mainnet you would need real tokens to distribute.

What happens if I lose access to my email?

Your wallet is tied to the Privy account created during email login. Use Privy's account recovery options. Your on-chain agreements are separate from your login — they will always exist on the blockchain.

Can the recipient see the agreement before accepting it?

Yes. All agreements are public on the Solana blockchain. Anyone with the agreement address can read it. No acceptance is needed — the recipient just visits the Agreements page with their own wallet to see and withdraw from agreements made for them.

Can I send tokens to multiple people in one agreement?

No — each agreement has exactly one recipient. Create separate agreements for each person.

What does "on-chain" mean?

It means the agreement is stored on the Solana network — a global, public, tamper-proof database — not on Vestra's servers. Even if Vestra's website went offline, your agreement would still exist and the recipient could still withdraw.

What is a wallet address?

A unique identifier for a blockchain account, like a bank account number. It is safe to share publicly. Example: 7xKXtg2CW87d97TXJSDpbD5jBkheTqA83TZRuJosgAsU.
