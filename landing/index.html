Vesting types
01
Standard
Cliff Vesting

Recipients receive nothing during the cliff period. Once the cliff passes, tokens vest linearly for the remainder of the schedule. The standard model for team and contributor agreements.

02
Continuous
Linear Vesting

Tokens vest at a constant rate from day one through to the end of the schedule. No waiting period. Every day counts from the moment the stream is created. Simple and predictable for both sides.

03
Conditional
Milestone Vesting

Tokens unlock in tranches when predefined conditions are met. Each milestone triggers a release. Ideal for project-based contributors where delivery matters more than time spent.

How it works

Three steps from setup to payout

01

Create a stream

Enter a recipient wallet, a token amount, and the vesting period. No technical knowledge required. The interface is as simple as filling out a form.

02

Tokens vest on schedule

Smart contracts release tokens over time according to the schedule you defined. Fully automatic. No manual transfers, no reminders, no spreadsheets.

03

Recipients claim anytime

Contributors connect a wallet and withdraw whatever has vested at any time, without waiting for your approval. Entirely self-service on their end.

What is token vesting?

Vesting is a way to distribute tokens gradually over time rather than all at once. A contributor might be entitled to 10,000 tokens over two years, receiving a portion each month as they continue contributing. It is standard practice in both traditional equity compensation and token-based organizations.

Do I need to understand blockchain to use this?

No. Themis is designed to feel like a standard web application. You will need a Solana wallet to sign transactions, but the interface does not require any knowledge of how the underlying protocol works.

What happens when a stream is cancelled?

Tokens that have already vested remain with the recipient and can still be claimed. Any tokens that had not yet vested are returned to the wallet that created the stream. The cancellation is instant and settled on-chain.

What is a cliff period?

A cliff is a waiting period before any tokens begin vesting. A one-year cliff means the recipient receives nothing for the first year, then vesting starts. This is common in contributor agreements to ensure commitment before rewards are distributed.

What tokens does Themis support?

Themis supports any SPL token on Solana, including USDC, wrapped SOL, and any custom project token that follows the standard SPL token format.
